# DALIA Governance Model
This document outlines the governance framework for the DALIA project. To ensure the standard remains neutral, adaptable, and community-driven, we employ a Minimum Viable Governance (MVG) structure.

## Roles and Responsibilities
The project includes the following roles:
* **Participants:** Anyone who engages with the DALIA community by attending meetings, opening issues, or joining discussions.
* **Contributors:** Participants who submit concrete changes to the standard or related tools (e.g., submitting Pull Requests or RFCs).
* **Maintainers:** Contributors who have demonstrated sustained engagement and are granted the technical rights to review and merge proposed changes.
* **Technical Steering Committee (TSC):** The core leadership group that guides the overall vision of the DALIA standard, resolves deadlocks, and ensures updates align with the core philosophy.

## Decision-Making Process
The DALIA Collaboration Space follows a Consensus-Seeking decision-making model.
For any substantial change to the standard's definitions, an RFC (Request for Comments) must be submitted. If consensus cannot be reached organically in the comments, the TSC will initiate a vote.
